# Goblin Arrows

## Overview

**Hook**: A series of goblin ambushes plague travelers on a forest road. A merchant offers a reward to anyone who can stop the raids and find the goblin lair.

**Party Context**: Level 3-4, 3-4 players, moderate experience

**Setting**: Forest road near settlements on Arrur (or any similar region); goblin camp hidden in dense woodland

---

## Chapters

### Chapter 1: The Ambush

The party encounters goblin archers while traveling a well-used forest road. They survive the initial attack and have the opportunity to pursue the attackers or gather information.

**Key Beats:**
- Party is ambushed by goblin archers (4-6 enemies total)
- Initial combat: goblins use hit-and-run tactics from range
- Goblins attempt to flee when outnumbered
- Party can pursuit, interrogate a captured goblin, or gather clues about a lair

**Pacing**: Fast and tense. Action-heavy opening to establish threat.

**Reward/Outcome**: 
- Captured goblin can be interrogated for lair location
- Tracks lead deeper into forest
- If goblins are completely defeated here, they may have map or note on them

---

### Chapter 2: The Pursuit

The party tracks the goblins back to their encampment. They discover the lair is larger and more organized than expected. Shrak, the goblin leader, has political ambitions and plans to expand his operation.

**Key Beats:**
- Investigation/tracking encounter (skill checks for following tracks)
- Scout the goblin camp from a distance
- Discover organization and crude defenses
- Choose approach: stealth infiltration, direct assault, negotiation attempt
- Confront Shrak and his forces

**Pacing**: Building tension. Investigation followed by final confrontation.

**Reward/Outcome**:
- Camp contains plunder from previous victims (merchants, travelers)
- Shrak may be captured alive (information about goblin hierarchy)
- Potential hooks for future campaigns (larger goblin movements, connection to bigger faction)

---

## Locations

### Location: Forest Road (Ambush Site)

**Description**: A well-traveled forest path connecting settlements. Tall trees on both sides provide concealment for ambush predators. The road is muddy, with scattered rocks and fallen logs creating natural obstacles.

**Setting Details**: 
- Dappled sunlight through canopy creates shadow and light patches
- Dense vegetation flanks both sides of the road
- Uneven terrain with natural cover (fallen logs, rocks)
- Sounds of forest birds and rustling leaves
- Smell of damp earth and pine

**Faction Control**: Neutral (no organized faction, but increasingly controlled by goblin hunting parties)

**Key Areas**:
- **Main road surface** — 20 feet wide, muddy, relatively clear for travel
- **Left flank (north)** — Dense brambles and thick trees, goblin archer positions
- **Right flank (south)** — Similar terrain, secondary goblin positions
- **Pursuit route** — Tracks leading away from road, gradually steeper into forest

**Sensory Details**: Natural forest ambiance becomes ominous as arrows suddenly rain down. Cries of alarm from travelers. Acrid smell of fletched arrows. Screams of wounded.

---

### Location: Goblin Camp (Lair)

**Description**: A ramshackle encampment hidden deep in the forest. Crude shelters made from branches and salvaged materials cluster around a central campfire. Lookout posts guard the hidden entrance. This is Shrak's growing domain—organized relative to typical goblin chaos.

**Setting Details**:
- Hidden behind thick brambles and fallen logs (natural camouflage)
- Central campfire provides light at night
- Scattered equipment and captured plunder visible
- Crude wooden palisade fence around perimeter
- Lookout platforms in nearby trees
- Smell of smoke, unwashed goblins, and animal hides
- Sounds of goblin chatter, eating, crude music/instruments

**Faction Control**: Shrak's Goblin Outfit (independent, ambitious, brutal)

**Key Areas**:

#### Area 1: Hidden Entrance
- Concealed opening behind thick brambles
- Single lookout post elevated in a tree
- Goblin guard rotates hourly
- Perception DC 14 to spot before being detected

#### Area 2: Main Encampment
- Central area with campfire
- 6-8 crude shelters (tents made of hides and wood frames)
- Where most non-combat goblins congregate
- Plunder stacked in corner: bolts of cloth, spoiled food, coin purses, equipment
- Easiest place to ambush multiple goblins at once

#### Area 3: Shrak's Tent
- Larger central tent, reinforced structure
- Contains Shrak's personal plunder and trophies (skulls, weapons, jewelry)
- Comfortable by goblin standards (furs, fire-pit)
- If Shrak retreats here, he makes a stand

#### Area 4: Perimeter Palisade
- Crude 8-foot wooden fence
- Lower on southern side (climbable, weak spot)
- Several gaps where vigilance has lapsed
- Wooden gate on eastern side (not locked)

## NPCs

### Shrak (Goblin Boss)

**Role**: Antagonist / Primary Villain

**Faction**: Independent Goblin Outfit (self-proclaimed leader)

**Archetype**: Goblin Warlord (equivalent to a martial warrior with tactical sense)

**Appearance**: Green-skinned goblin, lean and scarred, stands 4 feet tall. Missing left eye (scarred over). Wears salvaged dented militia armor and decorative bone accessories. Carries a well-maintained crossbow and a crude sword.

**Personality**: Snarling, guttural voice, speaks broken Common with goblin accent. Laughs at violence and suffering. Shows respect only to strength. Ambitious but not strategically sophisticated—prone to overconfidence.

**Goal in Adventure**: Expand goblin operations, establish Shrak as a legendary figure among goblin tribes, accumulate wealth and power. Views the party as a threat to his growing enterprise.

**Motivation**: Wants to prove himself as a leader; dreams of founding a larger goblin kingdom. Aggressive expansion mindset.

**Mechanical Details**: Proficient with crossbow (ranged attacks), skilled in melee combat but not exceptional. Lacks formal tactical training but uses terrain well. ~20-25 HP equivalent. Moderate armor (salvaged militia gear).

**Relationships**: Respected/feared by his goblin followers. Rivals with other nearby goblin bands. May have connections to larger goblin movements (hooks for future adventures).

---

### Kerrith (Merchant, Questgiver)

**Role**: Quest-giver / Ally

**Faction**: Independent merchant, affiliated with trade caravans

**Archetype**: Merchant / Common folk

**Appearance**: Middle-aged human, rotund, weathered face from travel. Wears travel-stained silks and practical leather armor. Nervous disposition.

**Personality**: Anxious about the goblin threat but genuinely grateful for help. Speaks quickly when nervous. Knows the roads well and has good local contacts. Offers fair payment and genuine gratitude.

**Goal in Adventure**: Stop the goblin raids so trade can resume safely on the forest road. Willing to pay above-market rates for guaranteed results.

**Motivation**: Protecting his livelihood and the safety of fellow merchants. Has lost colleagues to goblin arrows.

**Connection to Party**: Possibly encountered during the ambush (could be a victim they rescue) or met afterward seeking help. Has information about goblin activity and merchant routes.

**Reward**: 50-100 gold coins (negotiable), potentially future employment, safe passage through his trade networks.

---

### Mara (Goblin Scout / Optional Prisoner)

**Role**: Complication / Information Source (if captured)

**Faction**: Shrak's Goblin Outfit

**Archetype**: Goblin Scout / Archer

**Appearance**: Smaller than Shrak, nimble, quick reflexes. Has distinctive green tattoos on face (clan marks). Wears leather armor reinforced with bone plates.

**Personality**: Cowardly when cornered, boastful in safety. Quick-witted. Will betray Shrak if threatened with death, but may lie or mislead.

**Goal in Adventure**: Survive the encounter. If captured, wants to save own life by giving information.

**Information if Interrogated**: 
- Location and layout of goblin camp
- Shrak's rough power level and tactics
- Number of goblins in camp (~10-12)
- Which goblins are scouts, warriors, or non-combatants
- Rumor of larger goblin movements in the region (hook for future)

## Organizations

### Shrak's Goblin Outfit

**Type**: Organized goblin raider band

**Purpose**: Coordinated banditry and resource acquisition; Shrak's bid for power and status

**Structure**: 
- Shrak (leader, supreme authority)
- 2-3 lieutenant-type goblins (scouts and fighters)
- 6-8 warrior/archer goblins
- 2-3 non-combatant goblins (crafters, cooks, slaves)

**Relationship to Major Factions**: Independent, but may be part of larger goblin movements on Arrur (connection to be revealed later). Not yet organized enough to challenge established factions.

**Involvement in Adventure**: Primary antagonist. Their ambushes drive the plot. Defeating Shrak breaks this particular threat but hints at larger goblin political activity.

## Combat Encounters

### Encounter 1: Ambush on the Road

**Location**: Forest Road (Ambush Site)

**Objective**: Survive the goblin ambush and either defeat the attackers or force them to retreat. Secondary objective: capture/interrogate goblins for information about lair.

**Antagonists**:
- **Goblin Archer Captain** (leader): Slightly more skilled, gives commands
- **4x Goblin Archer**: Light armor, shortbows, quick reflexes, cowardly individually but coordinated
- **2x Goblin Bruiser**: Larger goblins with clubs and leather armor, aggressive, try to close distance for melee

**Complications**:
- **Ambush surprise**: Goblins have surprise if party isn't actively watching for trouble
- **Civilians present** (optional): A merchant caravan caught in crossfire (rescue complication)
- **Terrain**: Dense forest provides cover for enemies; arrows rain from multiple angles (difficult targets)
- **Retreat**: If goblins take 50% casualties, they scatter and flee into forest (gives party chase opportunity)
- **Captured goblins**: Any goblin captured alive can be interrogated for lair information

**Tactics**: 
- Archers target from range, use trees for cover
- Bruisers rush melee combatants to disrupt formations
- Flee when outnumbered; goblins prioritize survival over victory

**Locale Description**: 
The forest road narrows ahead as ancient trees press in from both sides. Dappled sunlight creates a patchwork of light and shadow across the muddy path. Suddenly, whistles pierce the air—then arrows! Goblin archers rain projectiles from the high branches to the left (north side), while larger figures crash through the right-side brambles. The air fills with the twang of bowstrings, the thud of arrows striking wood and leather, and the guttural yells of goblin voices. Fallen logs provide some cover, but retreat back down the road is partially blocked by debris. The sounds of panicked merchant animals (if present) add to the chaos.

---

### Encounter 2: Goblin Camp Battle

**Location**: Goblin Camp (Main Encampment & Surrounding)

**Objective**: Defeat or capture Shrak and his forces. Secondary objectives: rescue any captured prisoners, secure plunder/evidence.

**Antagonists**:
- **Shrak (Goblin Warlord)**: Level 4 equivalent, crossbow (ranged), sword (melee), tactical leader, ~24 HP
- **2x Goblin Captain/Lieutenant**: Skilled fighters, decent armor, ~15 HP each
- **6x Goblin Warriors**: Mixed archers and melee, ~10 HP each, organized but individually weak
- **Optional: 2x Non-combat Goblins**: Slaves or crafters, flee combat, not combatants

**Complications**:
- **Hidden entrance**: Goblins may ambush party from multiple angles if camp is well-scouted
- **Central campfire**: Bright light at night attracts attention but illuminates combat area
- **Palisade perimeter**: Party must decide whether to assault from a distance or breach perimeter
- **Shrak's tactical retreat**: If combat turns against Shrak, he retreats to his tent and makes final stand
- **Goblin reinforcements**: If camp sentries sound alarm early, goblins organize defenses faster
- **Collateral damage**: Adjacent shelters can catch fire if combat is intense
- **Shrak's escape**: Shrak may attempt escape through forest if he perceives certain defeat (party can track him)

**Tactics**:
- Shrak uses crossbow from range, then engages in melee with leadership
- Lieutenants coordinate goblin positions
- Warriors form loose formations around Shrak
- If party breaches perimeter, goblins rally behind palisade
- Shrak encourages final stand in his tent if surrounded

**Developments**:
- If party is clearly winning early, some goblins flee (reduces enemy count)
- If combat drags on, sentries alert goblins outside camp (reinforcements arrive in 2-3 rounds)
- Shrak's tent contains plunder and trophies (treasure to be claimed after victory)

**Locale Description**: 
The goblin camp sprawls across a forest clearing, camouflaged by tangled brambles and fallen logs. Crude hide-and-wood shelters cluster chaotically around a smoking central campfire. A rough palisade fence (8 feet high) surrounds the perimeter, with gaps visible where maintenance has lapsed. The air reeks of smoke, unwashed bodies, and animal hides. Snarling goblins pour out of shelters as alarms sound. Shrak stands fierce in firelight, his crossbow raised, scarred face twisted in rage. Warriors grab weapons and organize behind the palisade. The smell of combat—sweat, blood, fear—mingles with smoke. Overhead, branches creak in the wind. The forest beyond the camp is dark and impenetrable. Every goblin death brings cheers or cries; the encampment becomes a maelstrom of violence and confusion.

## Plot Hooks & Threads (Optional - For Future Adventures)

- **The Ring**: The mysterious ring found in Shrak's tent has goblin runes. An NPC expert identifies it as a sigil of rank or allegiance. Who gave it to Shrak? What larger goblin organization is he part of?

- **Larger Goblin Movement**: Captured goblins mention rumors of a "great goblin gathering" or unified tribes. This hints at a larger threat forming on Arrur.

- **Merchant's Debt**: Kerrith owes the party a serious favor. He can provide resources, information, or connections for future adventures.

- **Shrak Escapes** (if he flees): The goblin warlord swears revenge. He could become a recurring rival, building power for a rematch.

- **Political Complications**: Local nobility or merchants may have been financing goblin threats (blackmail/protection racket). Investigation reveals deeper conspiracy.

## Notes for the DM

- **Pacing**: This is a two-session adventure: 1st session = ambush encounter + investigation, 2nd session = camp assault & resolution
- **Difficulty**: Moderate for Level 3-4 party. Adjust goblin numbers for party size/power level
- **Tone**: Gritty and straightforward. Goblins are organized enough to be dangerous, but not sophisticated
- **Flexibility**: Goblins can be negotiated with (Shrak respects strength), though he's evil-aligned and unlikely to make peace
- **Non-lethal**: Party can capture Shrak alive for interrogation or turn him over to local authorities (legal reward)
- **Aftermath**: Clearing goblins opens travel road, grants local reputation, provides leads to larger threats

---

## System Notes

- **Creature Stats**: Adjust goblin HP and combat mechanics to match your system (D&D 5e, Pathfinder, homebrew, etc.)
- **Treasure**: Scale rewards based on party wealth and item availability
- **Encounters**: These are designed for Party Level 3-4. For higher levels, increase goblin numbers or add magical threats
